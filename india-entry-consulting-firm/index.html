How do we Register Our Business Name in India? 

Step 1

Prepare a list of possible names for your company: To get registered, it must have a unique name approved by the RoC. To be on the safer side, one should prepare a list of at least four names of the company that seems preferable so that if one of the names get rejected due to unavailability or any other dispute that offends Indian clients or authorities, one of the other names can be used for the registration process. It is a crucial step since the company’s name can attract more clients in the Indian market.

Step 2

Verify online to see if your preferred choice of the company name is available: – Before applying to register the company, one should ensure that the name selected is available for the registration process to go smoothly and not get hindered. The Indian Ministry of Corporate Affairs website can check whether other companies are registered under a particular name.

Step 3

Apply online with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs for the DIN: – you need a director identification number before registering your company. This unique number, provided by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, identifies your company’s current or intended director in India. You can apply for DIN online on the Ministry of Corporate Affairs website, which would require a passport, proof of address, and other documents.

Step 4

Apply and register online for a DSC (Digital Signature Certificate). A digital signature certificate is the digital equivalent of physical certificates that can be presented to prove your identity, tap into information or services on the Internet, or sign particular documents digitally. You can apply for DSC online on the same website as of DIN number.

Step 5

Compile your application materials and documents. After you’ve checked company names and obtained your DIN and DSC, you can fill out the company’s registration application online with the RoC.

Step 6

File E-Form 1A online to apply for the company name. The RoC will further approve or reject the company name based on availability and appropriateness in the matter of two days after you have paid Rs 500 for filling the form.

Step 7

You can further draft and prepare MOA and AOA documents and pay the registration and filing fees online for additional company registration. Paying these prescribed fees at the Ministry of Corporate Affairs’ website is the last step you need to complete before you can file your application for registration with the RoC. After gathering all the documents and filing for application one with RoC and paying the registration fees, you will receive the company’s certificate of incorporation.

  • Founders’ agreement
  • PAN Card of the company
  • Certificate of incorporation
  • No objection certificate (NOC)
  • GUSTIN
  • TIN 
  • Non-disclosure agreement
  • Employee offer letter

Do We Need a Resident Indian to be a Director in an Indian Company?

The company law of India does not hold any restrictions for foreign nationals from becoming directors in any Indian company. Therefore, a foreigner or any non-resident Indian can be an executive or a non-executive/independent director of any Indian company, irrespective of it is public or private.

What are the Benefits of Starting a Business in India for a Foreigner?

With an enormously diverse population of more than one billion and still increasing, India offers companies a large, established, and dynamic potential market to tap into. Some of the factors/benefits that India offers to any foreign national to start a business can be seen as: –

Diverse Population

A large population and diversified market with established logistics can be seen as one of the significant advantages of forming a startup business in India. India’s population continues to be a magnet for foreign companies.

Comprehensive Tax System

India enjoys a comprehensive network of tax treaties that ensures a smooth business procedure. Recently Indian tax system has been improvised by the Direct Taxes Code and the Goods and Service Tax (GST) to offer ease of doing business.

Business-friendly Laws

In the last few years, several important bills like the Companies Bill, Direct Taxes Code Bill, Goods, and Services Tax Bill, Land Acquisition Bill that are beneficial for most industrial sectors have been passed in the Indian Parliament. These business-friendly laws have made it easy for international players to actualize their plans of entering India.

Reduced Operational Cost

There is a significantly reduced cost of operation possible from infrastructures to labor to salaries to anything essential for setting up a business. Moreover, workers in India are willing to work at low salaries. Further, the tax strategies of India, when compared to other countries, are very moderate and hence could reduce the cost of operation of the business.

Indian Financial System

India has a well-regulated financial system that has access to established markets all over the globe and which can be financed through various sources restricted to some rules and regulations of RBI etc.

Impressive Trade Network

India flaunts an extensive network of technical and management institutions supported by regional and bilateral free trade agreements of the highest international standards. All in all, it offers an impressive pool of human resources.

Work Ethics and Working Class of India

Indians have their work ethics appreciated all over the world. The perfect blend of hardworking nature with the willingness to learn and never to say any attitude has distinguished Indians from their South Asian counterparts. With not just that, India has a large population fitted in the working-age group (18-65) which increases years of service availability in the Indian market. The youth is now looking for opportunities to invest in. Businesses can grasp this opportunity to the best of their use.

Government’s initiatives

The government of India has taken significant steps and initiatives to promote and attract foreign investments in India’s industrial sector by introducing numerous schemes and policies now and then to lure investments. It has also eased the rules and regulations for foreign investment in the market.

Startup India Movement

With the ‘Startup Inda Movement,’ the government has introduced several reforms to open possibilities for getting Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and foster business partnerships. Some initiatives have already been taken into consideration and action to relieve the business environment of outdated policies and regulations.
