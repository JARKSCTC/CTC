Frequently Asked Questions

What Does a Startup Business Consultant Do?

A startup business consultant is responsible for evaluating and assessing the company’s business strategy and further providing counselling over management and execution of resources and contingency plans for potential threats. The functions or responsibilities of the consultant can include: –

  • Offering an unbiased and experienced perspective and opinions on the business idea.
  • Evaluating company’s capabilities/abilities for future opportunities as well as threats
  • Preparing a realistic budget for business 
  • Conducting in-depth market research for competition, audience and strategies
  • Providing aid with plans by planning marketing activities, customer approach strategies, expansion strategies
  • Defining management framework for day-to-day performance of the organization.
  • Mentoring and training the staff to speed up business processes and help your company achieve its goals.

Why Do Startups Need a Business Consultant?

With the changing dynamics in the market platform, it is not uncommon for you to not know what will work for the business and what will put it in the new market. Every person in business introduces a business concept to succeed in the market and not for it to have a mere existence in the market. Here are some of the reasons for you to consider hiring a business startup consultant for your businesses: –

Selecting the appropriate business idea and assessing risks

The business idea must be soundproof, practical, scalable, implementable, and unique to work wonders among the audience. A business consultant helps you evaluate your business ideas and identify potential threats to the plan so that you invest only when you have the right plan to invest in.

Finalizing the business strategy and the road-map

Business consultants with their expertise, knowledge and experience play a significant role in finalizing the business plan for your startup. With years of experience and working with different people, these consultants tend to have an insight over business strategies and hence the skill to hone it according to the market environment.  

Efficient utilization of time, funds and efforts

A business consultant provides you with a strategy that requires only the necessary resources and funds to execute it. In addition, they ensure that any wastage of resources, funds or time is prohibited at all costs, thus saving the company’s resources that could be utilized at the time of need.

Handling complex situations

As a startup, you shall face challenges daily – more so on certain days than others. As you may face several difficult situations for the first time, tackling these situations can be overwhelming and exhausting. It may even inject you with the thought of giving up on your startup. In these situations, having a business consultant for your startup may prove to be a boon. They are likely to play an essential role in overcoming those problematic situations. They may have come across similar situations while working with other businesses or may know the right people who have been in similar situations and can help you out. Under challenging circumstances, a business consultant can prove to be an invaluable asset for your startup owing to their knowledge, skills, expertise and experience.

To steer the ship in the right direction.

In today’s ever-changing environment, there is a lot of chaos in the market and information bombardment, making experts have divergent views, which consequently may confuse you as an inexperienced founder of a startup and might lead you to make wrong or ill-informed decisions, which may prove fatal for the business. Therefore, the business consultant plays a significant role to ensure that well-informed decisions are made and things go in a planned and organized manner, cutting through the market chaos.

What Skills do You Need to be a Business Consultant?

To make an excellent, efficient startup consultant, one should possess these skills and traits to excel in their career and have the opportunity to work with big label companies. These essential qualities are: –

Bringing an experience to the table

You can consult only when you have the expertise over the subject matter, so quite evidently, a consultant should have the experience and skill set for the particular topic and subject matter they will give advice over. It would help if you worked yourself enough to brand yourself as a business consultant.

  • showcase transparency and hence trust

The business consultant has the responsibility of consulting and assessing the current working of the business because of their job designation and description; however, if you want to be a successful consultant, you should possess qualities like transparency, humbleness, professionalism, easy working attitude for people to trust your decision and suggestion.

Inherit the skill of being an Excellent COMMUNICATOR

As a business consultant, your job is to evaluate and assess a problem and offer a customized remedy for it. To do that efficiently, you must have good listening and communicating skills in your pocket to better understand the problem.

  • Consistency

A consultant’s work doesn’t just provide expertise for the business plan. They are also responsible for working on the plan and ensuring smooth execution. So, it becomes imperial for the consultant to show up with their A-game every day. You need to ensure that you are available whenever there is a need for you to be there. So, you show up every single time and bring on consistency.

Professionalism

A good business consultant knows when to stop being the parent and let the child be on his way (the company in this case). Likewise, you should know when to walk away and let your clients leverage your work; however, they see fit and not stick to it due to emotional investment in the project.

Ability to build LONG-TERM WORK CONNECTIONS

An intelligent business consultant knows that having an enriched source of connections is as important as consulting expertise. Having the right kind of people can bring you the correct result at the right time. Also, working with different professionals makes you learn their positives, which will step up your game compared to your competitors.

Flexibility

As a consultant, you need to be where the client is. Travelling is the silent factor in the job description of a business consultant. You should be able to commute by different modes of transport even if it is quite the distance. The most successful business consultants enjoy or, at the very least, tolerate the high volume of travel the job necessitates.

What Questions Should a Business Consultant Ask?

Here are some of the most asked questions:

  1. What is the result you want for your business?
  2. On a scale of 1 to 10, with ten being that you have arrived at the destination you desire. Where are you now?
  3. What will your action plan be if nothing happens to get you to your desired result in the next 12 months?
  4. What are the complexities keeping you from your desired result right now?
  5. What is the history of the situation (current situation or history of company’s working)?
  6. What are some of your action plans that you have tried to rectify or improve the situation?
  7. What factors affecting this situation are likely to change over the next ‘x’ years?
  8. What are the primary symptoms and the current risks related to the current situation? Etc.

How Much Does a Consultant Charge For a Business Plan?

A professional business plan consultant generally charges between $5,000 and $20,000 for a complete business plan depending on the scale and size of the business plan and the resources and time invested in it. For example, suppose the business plan demands the involvement of new or complicated technology, unusual revenue streams, or requires significant investment capital. In that case, the fees for the consultant can be at the higher end of the scale.
